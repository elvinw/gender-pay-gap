6 million more for losing in the first round

That is how much more the men's US World Cup team made compared to the $2 million prize money that the women's team won after becoming world champions. Every industry suffers a gender pay gap, but there is no room for women to speak out.


Much progress has been made since Lilly Ledbetter was denied her compensation in damages due to sexual discriminiation and harassment. However, there is still room for improvement in accountability. President Barack Obama signed the Ledbetter Fair Pay Act of 2009 and strengthened it in January 2016 by ordering the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission to collect summary pay data from companies larger than 100 employees.


Everyone has a woman in their life. The bad news is that some don't even know that they are a victim of pay discrimination That's why the challenge of closing the pay gap is to get everyone on board. And nobody, male or female, enjoy losing a piece of what they deserve.

The Pay Gap hurts everyone

Decades of research shows that no matter how you evaluate the data, there remains a pay gap — even after factoring in the kind of work people do, or qualifications such as education and experience — and there is good evidence that discrimination contributes to the persistent pay difference between men and women. Pay discrimination is a real and persistent problem that continues to shortchange American women and their families.
